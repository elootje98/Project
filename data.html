On this map you can see the percentage of smokers in every country. It looks like a high percentage of the population in Europe smokes tabacco daily. Also the country with the highest percentage lies in Europe: Greece (35.2%).

Click on the buttons and see how the map changes color. When clicking on 'women' the map gets filled with much brighter colors. Overall, women smoke less than men. Some experts say that the amouth of women that smoke compareed to the males in that country, says something about the emancipation of that country. Observe how country colors change when switching between women and males. You will see that Western countries don't change that much in color. Nevertheless, countries like for example, Indonesia, change from yellow to dark red. In conclusion: in Western countries women contribute more to the total percentage of smokers than in other countries.

Click on some countries on the map and see how the line graph changes. In the drop-down menu you can choose different kind of taxes. Take note of the fact that in Europe the hight of the taxes vary a lot between the countries. Greece, the country with the highest smokers percentage, has a total tax of 12.00%. However, they drastically rose this value to 42.90% in 2014. Be aware of the fact that the values in the map are from 2013, so the value of taxes in 2014 won't have effected the percentage of smokers on the map! If higher taxes would have an effect on the percentage of smokers, you would expect high taxes in countries with few smokers, and the other way around. However, for example in Germany there are relatively many smokers(24.2%), but the taxes are also high(35.80%). The oposite is seen in Mexico, with a smokers percentage of 8.3% and only 15.40% total tax.

When clicking on a country on the map, also the spiderchart changes. On the axes the types of advertising bans are displayed. You would expect that in countries with little advertising for tabacco, there would be less smokers. This can be seen in Panama (3.9% smokers), where no advertisement is allowed. It's also interesting to see that Panama has a total tax of 0% on cigarettes. Hover, in Jordan, where there is also no advertisement allowed, has a smokers percentage of 31.0%.

In this scatterplot the effect of taxes on the percentage of smokers is shown. It seems that taxes don't have an effect on the percentage of smokers. For example, in countries where there are no taxes on sigarettes, the percentage of smokers varies between 3.9% and 27.8%. However, the counry with the highest taxes (Singapore with 67.30%), has a low percentage of smokers(13.2%). Hover of the dots to see the exact value and the country they belong to.

Here the relation between the amouth of advertising bans and the percentage of smokers is shown. A strong correlations between advertising bans and taxes can not be seen.
